Research Spaces & Portfolio Design

2024 — 2026 · First author

From the research space to strategic portfolio design: forecasting academic diversification at the country level.

Fig. 1 — Research space · subfields by domain and proximity

Description

A study examining whether research-space analysis can ground the design of scientific portfolios from country–subfield forecasts. From a bibliographic catalog of articles, authors, and institutions, a research space is built from each country's activity and used to test whether measures derived from it help predict future shifts in specialization.

Method

An empirical framework that articulates three pieces: historical validation of research-space measures, comparison of alternative specifications of relative relatedness, and portfolio selection under an explicit complexity objective. Reduced-form stepping-stone models for subfield entry and exit are estimated, evaluated with in-sample evidence, leave-one-year-out validation, and robustness comparisons.

Result

In a 2024 comparison across 164 countries, the optimized portfolios depart sharply from relatedness-ranked baselines: 92.7% of countries shift toward lower auxiliary volume and higher forecast sophistication.

02

OntoDup

2024 — 2026 · First author

Governed entity deduplication over academic knowledge graphs.

Fig. 2 — Governed assertion with provenance and evidence

Description

An entity-matching pipeline for academic knowledge graphs that, rather than inserting direct links, represents every merge decision as a governed assertion carrying status, evidence, provenance, and operational metadata. The approach explicitly embeds principles of governance and traceability over matching results, aligned with the demands of explainable and auditable AI.

Architecture

Four layers: an ontological layer (vocabularies in RDF/Turtle), a rules layer in .pie format that materializes governed links, a candidate-pair generation and evaluation layer with multiple matchers, and a governance layer that emits assertions with provenance metadata. Evaluated on DBLP–ACM and DBLP–Scholar across four reproducible workflows.

Result

Experimental dataset published on Zenodo: 1.4 GB in N-Quads with results across four configurations, licensed CC BY 4.0 for reproducibility and graph-based inspection.

03

Social Media Topic Modeling

Jan — Oct 2025 · Co-author

Political discourse on social media during Ecuador's 2025 elections.

Fig. 3 — BERTopic pipeline and cluster visualization

Description

A study of the political discourse circulated on social media by Ecuadorian news outlets during the 2025 electoral cycle. It combines systematic data collection across four platforms, topic modeling with BERTopic, and an analysis of how dominant narratives evolved over time.

Approach

Collection of 8,174 posts from Facebook, Instagram, X, and TikTok drawn from 48 Ecuadorian media accounts, between January 1 and June 29, 2025, including engagement metrics (reactions, comments, shares, views).

Result

Public corpus of 8,174 posts with thematic clusters and temporal evolution, released on Zenodo (76.5 MB, CC BY 4.0). Presented at ETCM 2025 (Quito) and published in the IEEE proceedings.

05

CREAMINKA

May 2017 — Dec 2023 · Technical lead

An intelligent ecosystem for managing university knowledge and research.

Fig. 5 — Competency-evaluation model · CO ontology

Description

A comprehensive ecosystem designed to accelerate knowledge production, surface the value of research, and ease the identification of potential collaborators within universities, by way of ontologies and artificial intelligence. The cornerstone of my career: it spanned publications, book chapters, and operational deployments over more than six years at UPS.

Architecture

Four layers: a StartUPS transactional system for agent and competency data; a microservices component covering parser, authentication, CRUD, reporting, and analytics; an RDF triple store that models the ecosystem's knowledge; and mobile and web applications as the user interface. It includes a four-tier competency-evaluation model with self-, peer-, and external evaluation, plus mining over SCOPUS and Science Direct.

Result

  • Sustained analysis over more than 17,000 tuples in the ontological base.
  • Automated generation of resilience charts and reports for the Office of the Vice-Rector.
  • Identification of similar competency profiles across students from different programs.
  • An IntechOpen chapter with more than 1,300 recorded downloads.

SIRO

Oct 2015 — Jun 2017 · Developer

An intelligent robotic system supporting therapy for children on the autism spectrum.

Fig. 6 — Instrumented robotic assistant · Fundación CIMA

Description

A comprehensive technological-support system for the diagnosis and intervention of children with Autism Spectrum Disorder, developed with Fundación CIMA. My first professional foray into bridging ontologies with assistive technologies.

Approach

A hybrid architecture with three components: mobile tools for therapists and families, an instrumented multisensory room for intervention sessions, and a robotic assistant for mediation tasks. Knowledge about patients, therapies, and instruments is structured through ontologies that enable reasoning about progress and activity planning.

Impact

Beneficiary population: children on the autism spectrum, their families, and multidisciplinary teams across Special Education, Early Stimulation, Speech Therapy, and Psychology. Derived publications at IEEE ARGENCON 2016 (Buenos Aires) and CONIITI 2018 (Bogotá).
